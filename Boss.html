BEING BOSSY ISN'T ALWAYS BAD

Home Personal Bossy isn't negative

BOSSY IS NOT WHAT YOU THINK IT IS!

Let me preface this by saying, I will be taking into account how “bossy” varies per gender. So, if you are hurt or wounded later on in this blog, this was your warning, my friend. I can only speak from a personal point of view.

For a while now, I have been described as bossy. I am someone who takes initiative and is highly ambitious. Further, I am a control freak and get paranoid by just about anything that happens without my consent. Needless to say, the deadline is my best friend because it gives me a sense of control. I am sure many of you who tend to lean towards the assertive side would agree with me.

As a hormonal teenager, you are easily phased by others’ opinions about you and so was I, unfortunately. I was conditioned to think that the adjective ‘bossy’ was purely negative. I was told it was wrong and that it would lead to my downfall (not really certain what it meant to a teenager) and that I should learn to be more “humble”. I was told that “bossy” people are not appreciated in a household especially if it is a “bossy girl”.

I was told that people do not crush on you or approach you because you scare them away by being bossy. And for a teenager, that is a huge problem. I basically jumped to the conclusion that I was overbearing and probably never have a relationship of my own because, as everyone mentioned, I was bossy.

Slowly, this led me to lose a sense of me. You see, I thought it was more negative than it was made to be, which is again far from what it is. Studies have stated that at any workplace, a woman is twice as more likely to be called bossy than a man and if that does not indicate how terrible that is, there was an initiative that began with the aim to end the negativity named Ban Bossy. And that is when I realized there is a whole sphere of misunderstanding surrounding the word “bossy”.

I'M NOT BOSSY. I'M THE BOSS.

It is saddening to see how girls are negatively impacted by this stigma and lose themselves. They do not follow the role of leadership because they are afraid that they would be termed bossy. With the negative impact that surrounds the word, it becomes tougher to chase after “positions of power”. It is after a long time that I realized, the intent of a person differs.

To me now, bossy is not rude. It is just a word that determines that I am the leader and just as I think of my boss, people working under me would feel the same. It is not stubborn or arrogant. It is not pushy. It is just being assertive. It is being ambitious and it is being headstrong. It is every leadership skill you possess that might even be threatening to others!

So, if you are someone who has to let go of a part of them in order to fit the clique? Do not. Being you is what sets you apart. You are ambitious, headstrong, intelligent, and talented! And if one cannot differentiate between bossy and assertive, it is not your fault. You are all that you want and will to be. Oh, and yes, you are a boss!

Sending tons of love
M

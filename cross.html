At Crossroads.

Home Personal At Crossroads.

When I was 15, I decided I would be an engineer, not because my parents forced me to, but because they got paid well enough, and I saw a great example in my sister (Go little rockstar!). So naturally, my intermediate education was focused on MPC, and while it is not something I remember distinctly, I do remember grinding just to get into a college. And I did. More like paid to get a seat, but I did enter my engineering college. Three and a half years down the lane, I currently hold four job offers (not bragging), and I have a decent enough GPA to pursue higher education. Oh and yes, did I mention my 15 year old self also decided that she would pursue Masters and settle abroad with a high paying job? At 15, I knew my life was sorted. Sounds perfect, right? Absolutely NOT! Why did I have to try and figure out what it was that I was going to do at 15! Because those decisions have led me to the current dilemma I am in. At crossroads, with no clarity and admittedly a little anxiety over my entire future! Oh, if you are feeling the same, welcome to the gang, and please do read on to know that YOU are DEFINITELY NOT alone.

I am going to begin working as a full-time employee in a few months, and I have absolutely no idea if this field is something I even want to explore. But somehow I thought I would understand further when I pursued my Masters. Do you guys realize the major flaw in my plan? I was willing to invest heavy bucks into something I am half certain about. That is when I applied brakes on my Master's plans, well that, and also my plunging GPA and decided to work for a year in my core field and then decide if it is even something I see myself doing. Quite frankly, and definitely not in a cocky way, I am good at my core domain. But, I know I am better at other things. Writing being one of them. Upon deep introspection, I finally comprehend that the reason I have been staying away from blogging has also been due to the fact that I cannot possibly fathom the existence of a reality where writing might be a career option that can completely satisfy me. Ironically though, so far every source of income I have received is because of my writing, either as a freelancer or a part-time employee.

But, you might be wondering what triggered this sudden spiral? A couple of months ago, I was faced with this question which summed up to elucidating about my passion. Imagine my surprise and disgust when my answer was a simple “I don’t have any passion.” Could this be a lack of passion? Or straight up denial? I am mentally not in the state to comprehend the right answer, or maybe I am not in the state to accept it. But truth be told, I think we are all pressured into knowing and charting out our course of life for the minimum of next 10 years, and I personally believe that leads to people focusing on either monetary satisfaction, or self-satisfaction. I am not jealous of the people who have their life planned ahead. I am very happy for them, because this place isn’t great. The constant fear of failure, the anxiety of probably never finding what makes you truly happy, and obviously the need to be financially stable is daunting! Earlier today I came face-to-face with a certain shortlisted mail which should have thrilled me to the core, but apart from being scared, I also felt as if I was betraying someone. Don’t get me wrong, I know it was probably my hard work that got me to the current level, but I also believe that there are other people whose dream would be that role. And that was when I decided I would finish this blog. Because that weird feeling of snatching something from someone sucks and no one should feel that! I strongly believe that whatever it is that you receive is a fruit of your labor. So just because you think you aren’t passionate about something, and still manage to do well at it, chalk it up to the fact that you put in those efforts!

Lacking passion, lacking clarity, lacking… Well everything… I still managed to survive, and I will continue to survive, and I know you will too. I still haven’t made a choice. I have an internship lined up, followed by a couple of jobs in my core domain. I still have a freelance gig for writing, on my profile. I know I cannot possibly get the best of both worlds, but I do know that I should have to feel the pressure to decide right this day. I think the fault has always been in the fact that I pushed myself to decide. I gave myself one right option, and one wrong. Somehow, I thought picking one would mean I could never do the other again in my life. Yet, I know if I continue writing, I can always pull up hackerearth, or hackerrank! If I choose coding, I can always write something on my blog. Even after having written all this myself, I still cannot possibly accept this, because advice is easier given than followed.

So, here it is. I am at a crossroads, with others that I know. But, I know it isn’t necessary for me to have everything sorted today. For now I will take it a day at a time, and still be anxious but also know that I can always decide, change, take a u-turn, run, and find my passion or what truly makes me happy one day, down the lane. That day may not be today, but it will be someday. Oh and if this blog was confusing, my brain is even fuzzier. But you can always rant, so hit me up on any of the mentioned socials!

Hoping you find clarity and happiness soon,
M.

Is 5G the most pressing issue in India?

The Internet has been one of the major factors of development, worldwide. The rate at which the technology is growing around is clearly astounding. Every day, there is a new addition or a whole new advent, being added to this growing ocean. One such advancement, which has brought about a prominent chitter chat along with it, is the 5G network. Yes, as the name suggests, it is indeed a step up from the 4G network, we are surrounded by, currently.

History

The 5G network would essentially mean, upgrading our devices and sim cards to ensure they are compatible with the network. But, here is what I feel might be an issue. When 4G was introduced initially in our country, which was quite late relatively across the world, the citizen had a tough time during this upgrade as most of them were clearly unaware of how to function a normal device. To introduce a whole new concept will certainly prove to be a hassle as the previous one has not been accepted to its full potential yet.

Turbulent times lie ahead

For a developing nation to adopt advancements, which are introduced late, in the first place is hardship when a part of the population has no knowledge regarding technology. Further, there are many villages that do not have service for the normal network, either. Change is slow progress. One cannot expect the plant to grow right after the seed is sowed. Moreover, just establishing the network will do no good. Making sure to provide devices with connection capability will be a trial that the entire nation will have to go through

Further, the need to have Spectrum is quintessential for establishing this 5G network. It is highly relieving to know that there will be an auction before the end of the year and yet, it is going to be a matter of concern as Spectrum is expensive. And, with several operators fighting to stay in the competition, by reducing their 4G price has drowned them in debts. While there is hope to pass these turmoils, other problems arise in terms of space.

With connectivity increasing, the towers will have to increase. But do we have enough space to accommodate it? That is a question that will have to be considered when we look at implementing 5G in our nation, effectively.

But we can get through it

While the aforementioned trials seem daunting, the result is something that will truly shape the ‘Digital India’ dream. To compete with nations in the tech field is no easy task. And if you want to be one of the front runners, you need all the armory you can gather. And, 5G might be the game-changer in this scenario. And, we are already far behind. 5G needs to be established but the foundations have to be strong to hold the bulk it will face.

But, once it is settled, it will multiply the potential in terms of network usage and more. It will initially be a rough start, with issues, just like any other networks, that came in before, but there is no doubt that 5G will stand the test of time and emerge victorious, benefitting the entire nation.

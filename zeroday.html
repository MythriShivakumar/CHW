HOW SECURE IS SECURE?

Home Professional Android is more secure?

Zero-day marketplace suggests Android might be more secure than iOS

Android versus iOS has been a never-ending feud. And when it comes to details like security, iOS has been miles ahead for various reasons. A major issue with Android’s security would be the open-source environment, which has made it vulnerable to attacks. Another contributing factor is that more than 90% of Android users run the outdated software meaning they are more prone to attacks because of the unresolved bugs.

On the other hand, iOS has never shied away from boasting all about its top-notch security features. Right from the App store which is almost free of any unsafe apps along with the encrypted messaging app, iMessage among various others, iOS has always stood out.

So why is the title of this article a contradiction?

Here’s why. There is a zero-day marketplace. For those unaware, it is basically a threat that has been discovered but there is no time to fix this vulnerability, thus the name zero-day, quite literally. During this time, there is a serious possibility of exploitation of data, spreading malware, and more. Chaouki Bekrar, the founder of the exploit acquisition platform Zerodium, phrases this as a supply-demand market.

If there is an increase in the zero-day exploits for a particular product, then the security level has decreased for the said product as the price has decreased due to the numerous exploits that are available.

So far, Zerodium has offered a high estimation for exploits in iOS when compared to Android because of the above-said vulnerabilities in the latter. But, here is the major game-changer. In September, Zerodium increased the payout for Android to 2.5 million dollars which were earlier capped to merely 500,000 dollars! And it was certainly surprising because it was more than the payout for exploits in iOS which stood at 1.5 million dollars.

This could lead to two conclusions. One being, Android has beefed up its security and is now going to give iOS a run for its money. And the other would be since there has been a huge amount of attention paid to iOS exploits thus increasing its supply and decreasing the prices. But various experts have attested to the fact that Android has certainly increased its security features and thus is entering a new league of its own. Further, Google has been working relentlessly on developing the safety features over the years, and finally reached a point from where everything just seems to be uphill.

Although, the same cannot be said for iOS. Recently, there has been a surge in the number of iOS exploits so much so that Zerodium had to refuse to take in more as they have been flooded! Moreover, in May, Zerodium has temporarily halted the purchase of iOS exploits because of the high number of submissions.

But, Ryan Narine, the director of the security strategy at Intel summed it up as marketing shenanigans. Yet, various others have also noticed the loopholes present in iOS security. While this tale of security is unfolding, it also brings to light the fact that 70% of iOS users have the latest version while not even 10% of the Android users can say the same. And if a threat is discovered all iOS has to do is send an update because Apple does not deal with multiple hardware.

Even though Android is constantly improving its security, the fact that most of the users have outdated software does not really bode well with the advancement. But, I certainly see it changing in the future. All we can hope for is the solution to this woe of Android to arrive early.

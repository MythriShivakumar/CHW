TIME TO UNINSTALL WHATSAPP?

Home Professional Time to uninstall WhatsApp?

WhatsApp’s New Policy.

Alike several WhatsApp users, I encountered a pop-up which meant I had to accept the new privacy policy. Hesitating to read the terms, due to various other works, I set it aside because I thought it’d be something minor. Then, this topic came up in conversation with friends and also across social media platforms. It naturally urged me to read up further about what is sending everyone into this frenzy. Why a change in the policy of an app would possibly be perilous. Then I realized how WhatsApp will now share your data with Facebook and other Facebook-owned apps, which includes but not only limited to Instagram.

If you are wondering what is your data that WhatsApp holds, let me explain it to you. The app has access to the basic information about your phone model, OS, IP address, WhatsApp usage along with Battery and signal strength. With the introduction of WhatsApp Pay, this messaging app collects payment and transaction details. While the messages are end-to-end encrypted, the application still holds group details, which includes the group picture, about, and also your profile picture. So basically, everything. And it has always had access this whole while. But, what is the change that has everyone question the presence of this app? All the details which WhatsApp had exclusive access to will now be shared with Facebook and all the third-party services. Your data is no longer as secure as it used to be. Which includes customized ads across Facebook and Instagram.

For example, if you are sending shopping links to a friend, be ready to find similar ads across your Facebook. The app still doesn’t allow third-party banners in the app but may have hinted towards it being introduced shortly. The new terms and conditions also mention that your data will be shared with the business accounts you interact with. Second questioning security? Same here.

What can you do about it?

Well, if you want to continue using Whatsapp, nothing. You have to accept the new terms and conditions. As opposed to the rumors, your conversations are still end-to-end encrypted. You may find ads similar to what you have been searching/sharing across this messaging app, similar to google search. If you don’t care much about the app, you can pick from the more secure alternatives that get the job done just as well. There is Telegram which acquired 25 million new users since the policy update. This messaging app has seen a genuine surge in users, now surpassing 500 million. I have used Telegram before and it didn’t quite satisfy me. Further, the app doesn’t allow end-to-end encryption by default.

Another app that I began using recently is, Signal. Extremely similar to WhatsApp but with added benefits like your data not being shared makes this an apt replacement. In fact, in the one week that I have been on Signal, the number of contacts joining the app has increased, too. Elon Musk himself tweeted ‘Use Signal’ post the controversy.

While this invasion of privacy is certainly outrageous, I find it being blown a little out of proportion. Frankly, your device is always spying on you just like the movie ‘The Social Dilemma’ explains (If you haven’t watched it yet, I strongly recommend you do.). The one case where I suggest you be extra cautious is while interacting with business accounts. When these policies come into action, you can assume WhatsApp to be another version of Messenger.

Apart from this, any news that state otherwise is completely false. Yet, if you still want your data to be secure you can choose from the alternatives mentioned above, or others. Overall, this is not a positive change. It does change the integral values of the app but, it was bound to be so, over time. It is not the first app to do so either. The rumors, stories, and memes dramatized it more than it ever was. In the end, you have to use what suits your purposes the best!
Safe browsing!

A CHIP LIKE NO OTHER?

Home Professional The M1 chip.

M1 chip in Macs.

While this may seem long overdue, due to my hiatus, I couldn’t help but praise this new addition to the tech field. In November 2020, Apple released the first set ever Macs with an Arm-based chip, M1. The company had mentioned it would be drifting away from Intel chips back in June 2020 and the debut of the M1 chip assuredly left a lasting impression with its improved performance.

The first of several changes that are to come in the future of this company, the M1 chip is a product that consists of all the best features from a decade worth of hard work put in by the tech giant in the chips created for iPhones and iPads. While the hype around the expectations was high going in, Apple didn’t miss the mark, if anything, raised that bar with premium work. It is also the most powerful chip Apple has created to date.

Transitioning between processors is certainly treacherous. There are numerous areas to fail and yet, this Cupertino company managed to complete this transition with ease. Being a System on Chip, the M1 includes the CPU, GPU, unified memory architecture (RAM), Neural Engine, Secure Enclave, SSD controller, image signal processor, encode/decode engines, Thunderbolt controller with USB 4 support, and more.

Earlier, Macs used different chips to serve different purposes like CPU, I/O, and security. But, with all of it being integrated into the M1 chip, it reduces the delay and makes the system more efficient. Further, with the unified memory, the system will be able to access the same data without having to swap memory pools.

The M1 chip has undeniably increased the speed of the system. The CPU performance has now increased by 3.5x and the GPU performance is now 6x faster and the machine learning capabilities are 15x times faster when compared to the previous Intel-based generation systems. When factoring in the battery life, the M1 chip allows the new generation of Macs to last 2x longer than the intel based past generation models.

With this optimal chip being an amalgamation of the various chips used in the prior models, the security is enhanced as well. Secondary chips are no longer required with the introduction of the M1 chip. While everything seems peachy, the webcam is still disappointing, and running iPad apps is not smooth. Further, third-party applications like Photoshop, Chrome, and more are still facing glitches as these are not native code applications rather translated through Rosetta 2. The company addressed and announced will be fixed with an update that is yet to happen.

While it seemed like there’d be only a single variant, Apple managed to deliver MacBook Air, MacBook Pro, and Mac mini. While this sounds exceedingly impressive, there doesn’t seem to be a stark difference between the MacBook Air with M1 chip and entry-level MacBook Pro with M1 chip, apart from the fan. While the latter will last longer due to the cooling, it cannot be said the same for the prior.

The small differences in display, sound, and more but when it boils down to performance, it can be considered as MacBook Air with a fan. Comparing to the model in 2019, it is an upgrade for sure. Yet, is the entry-level Pro a better investment than the Air? That is a question with no definitive answer.

The introduction of this Arm-based chip without a doubt made Macs power-efficient and faster. But, it also paved way for colossal changes that will now be made by this organization. So far, this chip is nothing but all praises. So much so that it can also be considered as the best product Apple has created in the year 2020.

Several reviewers also consider the MacBook with M1 to be the most impressive laptop they have used. Personally, with a dying laptop, I do have my eye on the Mac with M1, too. But, hey...Who doesn’t? The features are stellar, the performance is stunning! All in all, the M1 chip has increased the standards for every other processor and for Apple, itself.

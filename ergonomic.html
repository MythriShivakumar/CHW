WORK FROM HOME CULTURE

Amidst the COVID-19 pandemic, every company has rethought its entire process of workflow. This breakout has sparked a riot of thoughts within the employers as halting has never and will never be an option. Physically attending work was ruled out as soon as Governments imposed lockdowns in numerous parts of the world. If anything, this outbreak has revolutionized what we perceived to be ‘Attending work’.

It has been reported that companies are investing in creating workspaces at home, as the likes of Google, Uber, and Salesforce have either started paying allowances for work-from-home expenses or expanded the scope of existing allowances to include ‘Work From Home’ furniture. Provided that couches and dining table chairs are not the most ideal places to work from, to invest in furniture and workstations could mean that this culture of WFH is going to extend beyond the boundaries of this pandemic. It could now be the mode of work.

While ‘Work From Home’ has been a longstanding culture, it came with its restrictions such as a limited amount of days or sometimes not even having that opportunity for its power could have been abused. The tradition of going to an office was a whole different mode of socializing. It has been a method to bring team members closer and the fact that you could always keep an eye on the progress just by hovering behind the desk, was just a bonus. It did come with its own set of let downs which everyone had to buck up and face because having a job is better than having no job.

Maybe now ‘transferring’ will not mean moving to a whole new city anymore.

The word ‘transfer’ has always been sweet, bitter, or bittersweet depending upon the person. For someone with a career that just began, with fewer commitments to a place, it seems like a perfect opportunity to grow but for the ones with family, estates, and more, it is a change they are not happy with but have to agree with. Promotions and more are lost under the trance of not accepting a transfer. But maybe this is a whole new chapter for the ones who hated transferring.

Overall, to me, it seems like a change that was needed by the majority but at the same time, it holds the power to turn the tables on work ethics and colleague interactions. But, it does depend upon how one uses it. For now, it is a safe bet that this transition will not be smooth but will certainly be welcomed with a warm embrace.
